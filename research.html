Ongoing Research

AI-Based Detection of Drug Trafficking in Social Media

This research focuses on developing an intelligent AI system capable of detecting drug trafficking related communication across social media platforms. The system aims to identify hidden keywords, coded slang, emoji patterns, and suspicious contextual language using advanced Natural Language Processing techniques.

Research Problem Statement

Drug trafficking networks increasingly use encrypted and disguised communication on social media. Traditional keyword-based monitoring systems fail to capture contextual meaning and evolving slang terms. This research proposes a context-aware AI model to improve detection accuracy and reduce false positives.

Research Objectives

• Detect drug-related keywords and slang terms
• Identify contextual patterns in suspicious conversations
• Train classification models using machine learning
• Evaluate model performance using precision, recall and F1-score
• Develop a prototype monitoring system

Methodology

• Data Collection from public datasets and synthetic samples
• Text Preprocessing (tokenization, stopword removal, lemmatization)
• Feature Extraction using TF-IDF and Transformer embeddings
• Model Development (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, LSTM)
• Model Evaluation and Optimization

Expected Outcome & Future Scope

The expected outcome is a high-accuracy AI model capable of detecting suspicious drug-related content in social media posts. Future enhancements include multimodal detection combining text and image analysis, multilingual support, and real-time monitoring dashboard deployment.
